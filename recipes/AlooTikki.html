Aloo-Tikki

Ingredients:
  • 6 russet potatoes (boiled, peeled, cooled, grated)
  • 1 tsp kala jeera (roasted cumin or regular)
  • 2 tsp coriander powder
  • 2 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp chat masala (I like MDH)
  • 1 tsp Kashmiri red chili powder (or paprika)
Steps by step procedure to make aloo tikki:
  1. Boil potatoes.
  2. Once cooled, peel the potatoes.
  3. Grate the potatoes in a large mixing bowl.
  4. Prepare bread crumbs, I simply add a slice of whole wheat bread to my 4-cup mini food processor.
  5. Add all the spices and fresh cilantro to the grated potatoes
  6. Top with breadcrumbs.
  7. Using your hands, and mash all the ingredients well.
  8. Take a golf-sized amount of potatoes into the palm of your hand.
  9. Form round patties.
  10. Heat up a 10.25″ castiron skillet on low-medium heat for 3-4 minutes – you can cook up to 4 patties at a time. Once hot, add avocado oil and place the aloo tikki patties on the castiron.
  11. Cook each patty for 5-6 minutes on each side, and then flip and cook for 5-6 minutes on the other side.
  12. Cook the patties in batches. Once done turn off the stove.
  13. Place the cooked patties on a paper towel to help remove excess oil.
  14. Place patties on a plate.
  15. Top with yogurt, green chutney, and tamarind chutney.
  16. Top with chopped red onions fresh cilantro, and sev.
  17. Finish off with a sprinkle of chat masala and red chili powder.
